Global States of Consciousness

Global states are defined as a range of our different levels of awareness. They are another way to think about consciousness. Each state is a representation of a distinct pattern of brain activity, including congnition and responses. The states range from fully alert to vegitative states and coma's. Consciousness is compartmentalized into 6 categories, which are as follows:

Normal

The person is fully aware and conscious of their actions.

Locked-in Syndrome

The person is completely non-verbal and looks like they are in a vegitative state but are actually fully conscious. They exhibit no purposeful movements and can't respond in any way.

Minimally Conscious

The person shows some purposeful actions, and are sometimes aware.

Vegitative State

The person can be awake but not completly aware, and might not respond verbally.

Coma

The person might have some invoulentary reflexes, but no awareness at all. Being under anasthesia is very similar to being in a coma state.

Brain Death

The person has no motor function and no awareness, but it is possible to still be physically alive.
